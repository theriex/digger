Music Fans

A music fan is someone who enjoys listening to music. Digger is for music fans who collect songs and listen to them through the years. Music fans like to connect around music. They might not always agree, but they like learning what the other person appreciates. Fans like to connect around music.

Back in the day, when you went over to a someone's house, you might check out their album collection, see how much you recognize, check out what you don't recognize, and maybe talk about what you've been listening to recently. They might play something they think you should hear, or let you choose something to put on. This type of interaction didn't scale into the digital age the way radio and mixtapes did. There are several reasons:

  • Casually browsing other people's music collections doesn't work anymore because albums are no longer part of the room. They exist on screen.
  • Without the constraints of physical space, libraries have grown very large.
  • With a large collection, it takes more effort and knowledge to find something appropriate to suggest.

Digger makes it easier to access our collections, enjoy our music, and support music interactions like these:

Have you heard...

Some of the most treasured music in my collection came from people who pointed out gems I hadn't discovered yet. If they hadn't clued me in, I might have missed out on music I've listened to for years since.

I'll usually try find whatever was suggested if I haven't heard it yet. If it's good I'll add it to my collection.

Response

When I rate a song that was previously suggested to me, my impression becomes a response, provided I thought it was better than average. If I thought it was at best average, then it's probably better not to bother telling whoever suggested it that the song they like is not very good. They might have other suggestions that are much better. Or I might stop paying attention to them.

Discovery

When I rate a new song highly, and someone in my fan group has also rated it highly, Digger lets them know we now have that music in common even if they hadn't suggested it. That helps build our connection strength and helps them learn what kind of things I like.

Path cross

If I listen to a song, and in the past day or so someone else in my fan group has also listened to the same song, or another song from the same album, or the same artist, that can be fun to be informed about. It's a good excuse to get in touch with them and maybe connect more.

This song is amazing!

Sometimes when I listen to a song, it's just perfect at the time. I'll suggest it to others in my fan group who haven't heard it. Sometimes I'll copy my impression info and paste that into whatever social broadcast app I'm currently using. Or mail it to someone who isn't on Digger yet.

Notable artistic aspects

When I'm listening, sometimes I'm struck by aspects of the composition, performance, production, or other magic that is worth noting for my future self and anyone else I might be providing suggestions to. I record these impressions in the song comments when they occur to me while I'm listening.

Song stories

Songs are recorded in a studio or at a live performance, but sometimes there's more to it. Fans in the know often share amusing or illuminating anecdotes about the song background, contractual obligations, recording situations or other info. These anecdotes can also be put in the comment field, but there is an opportunity here to do more.

Artist connections

Great music is made by great musicians, and it can be rewarding to see what else they have done who else they have worked with. Some relationships are surprising and lead to things you might not have explored otherwise. Looking forward to developing this further.

Personal connections

Nothing beats directly supporting artists you like. "I saw them live" expresses both appreciation and commitment. Any connection to the creative process is great and worth sharing, including music purchases and other items that directly benefit artists. Most musicians don't get enough support from streaming. You should be able to see more of how your fans are connecting to the music.

Happenings

New releases, upcoming concerts, and other happenings are important to know about. It's a drag missing something you would have wanted to go see. Fans are good source, but looking forward to connecting with services to get the scoop on what's going on.

Help with setup

Fans help you get set up. When you add a fan to your group, Digger checks their library for songs you haven't rated yet and copies some of their impression info to jump start your tuned listening experience. When you rate your own music you help fans discover music they want to hear. All while you get back into enjoying music in your library.
