Music Fans

A music fan is someone whose musical listening interests and reactions you respect and enjoy. You might not agree on everything, but you definitely agree on some things, and you might share tips and reactions to various songs and artists.

Back in the day, when you went over to a someone's house, you might check out their album collection, see how much you recognize, check out what you don't recognize, and maybe talk about what you've been listening to recently. They might play something they think you should hear, or let you choose something to put on. This type of interaction didn't scale into the digital age like radio or mixtapes. There are several reasons for this:

  • Casually browsing other people's music collections doesn't work anymore because albums are no longer part of the room. They exist on screen.
  • Without the constraints of physical space, libraries have grown very large.
  • With a large collection, it takes more effort and knowledge to find something appropriate to suggest.

How we access our collections needs help to make it easier to enjoy our music and bring back fan interactions like the following:

Have you heard...

Some of the most treasured music in my collection came from fans that pointed out gems I hadn't discovered yet. If they hadn't clued me in, I might have missed out on music I've listened to for years since.

Digger supports suggestions of new music from fans. If a music source supports it, fan-suggested songs matching your current retrieval parameters can be included on deck. When you play a recommended song, it gets added to your collection with the ratings you set. You can activate up to 4 fans at once all providing suggestions of music to play.

This song is amazing!

Sometimes when you listen to a song or album, the experience is so great you need to let other fans know about it. If you have saved contact fan contact info with your account, Digger helps with the typing by filling out text about the song that is playing, and some of what's on deck, to provide context.

Notable artistic aspects

When you are listening, you might be struck by aspects of the composition, performance, production, or other magic that is worth noting for your future self and any fan you might be providing suggestions to. Put these notes in the song comment field to capture them.

Context

Songs are recorded in a studio or at a live performance, but sometimes there is more to it. Fans in the know often share amusing or illuminating anecdotes about the song background, contractual obligations, recording situations or other info. These anecdotes can also be put in the comment field, but there is an opportunity here to do more. Context might be more stories than facts, but it's always fun to hear from fans.

Relationships

Great music is made by great musicians, and it can be rewarding to see what else they have done who else they have worked with. Some relationships are surprising and lead to things you might not have explored otherwise. Looking forward to developing this further.

Personal connections

Nothing beats directly supporting artists you like. "I saw them live" expresses both appreciation and commitment. Any connection to the creative process is great and worth sharing, including music purchases and other items that directly benefit artists. Most musicians don't get enough support from streaming. You should be able to see more of how your fans are connecting to the music.

Happenings

New releases, upcoming concerts, and other happenings are important to know about. It's a drag missing something you would have wanted to go see. Fans are good source, but looking forward to connecting with services to get the scoop on what's going on.

Help with setup

Fans help you get started. When you add a music fan, Digger checks their library for songs you haven't rated yet and copies their rating info to jump start your tuned listening experience. When you rate your own music you help fans discover music they want to hear. All while you get back into enjoying music in your library.
