Music Fans

A music fan is someone who enjoys listening to music. Dedicated music fans collect music so they have it and can listen to it whenever they want. Digger is a tool to help dedicated music fans rate and retrieve music from their collections, and connect with friends around music.

Back in the day, when you went over to a friend's house, you might look over their album collection to see how much you recognize, check out what you don't recognize, and maybe talk about what you've been listening to recently. Your friend might play something they think you should hear, or let you choose something to play. As music transitioned into the digital age, concepts like radio and mixtapes adapted, but sharing around personal collections fell by the wayside.

Returning to those analog days is impossible. Even if we could somehow return, collections have grown from hundreds of albums into thousands. Looking through them all is starting to get a bit tedious. To move forward, we need to focus on the foundations of your collection. That is, your impressions of the music. More specifically, the aspects of your impression most important for collaboration and retrieval.

Digger is a rating and retrieval app providing automated access your collection based on your own impressions. Digger supports collaboration with friends and fellow music fans through DiggerHub.

Sharing and collaborating

Click the Digger "share" button to copy the song Title, Artist, Album, Keywords, Approachability and Energy Level, along with your saved personal comment. Send that to anyone any way you like, sharing great music improves anyone's day and helps them get to know you better.

You can also collaborate through DiggerHub.

1. Connect

Under the "fans" section of your profile, choose "connect" to see friends and fellow music fans you are following. To add a friend, search for them by their digger name. If you haven't added any friends yet, and you want to get a feel for how things work, click "Connect Me" to add whoever the hub picks for you.

2. Match

Like recognizing album covers in your friend's collection, "match" lets you see how many songs you and your friend have in common. If you have songs in your collection that you haven't rated yet, your friend provides default rating information you can use for contextual retrieval. The "rcv" tab shows how many default ratings you've received. The "snd" tab shows how many default ratings they have received from you. If you remove a friend, their default ratings are also removed. When you play a song, any default rating information for that song becomes yours.

3. Messages

See what your friends have shared recently and get automatic recommendations from your friends matching your current listening parameters.
