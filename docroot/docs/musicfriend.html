Music Friends

A music friend is a friend whose musical listening interests and reactions you respect and enjoy. You might not agree on everything, but you definitely agree on some things, and music is something you talk about.

Back in the day, when you went over to a friend's house, you would check out their album collection, see how much you recognize, check out what you don't recognize, and maybe talk about what you've been listening to recently. Your friend might play something they think you should hear, or let you choose something to put on. Radio and mixtapes were also key, but those adapted to the digital age while friend music interactions had a harder time.

Browsing music collections doesn't work anymore because they exist on screen, not as part of the room. Without the constraints of physical space, libraries can get very large. If you have a collection that has grown big, you'll notice it takes more and more effort to find what you want to play. How we access our collections needs help to make it easier to enjoy our music and bring back interactions with friends.

What are some of the types of friend interactions?

Have you heard...

Some of the most treasured music in my collection came from friends that pointed out gems I hadn't discovered yet. If they hadn't clued me in, I might have missed out on music I've listened to for years since.

Digger supports suggestions of new music from friends. If a music source supports it, friend-suggested songs matching your current retrieval parameters are included on deck. When you play a recommended song, it is added to your collection with the ratings you set. You can activate up to 4 friends at once all providing suggestions of music to play.

This song is amazing!

Sometimes when you listen to a song or album, the experience is so exceptional it must immediately be shared with friends who might also appreciate it. To help with the typing, Digger fills out text about the song that is playing, and some of what's on deck, to provide context.

Notable artistic aspects

When you are listening, you might be struck by aspects of the composition, performance, production, or other magic that is worth noting for your future self and any friend you might be providing suggestions to. Put these notes in the song comment field to capture them.

Context

Songs are recorded in a studio or at a live performance, but sometimes there is more to it. Friends in the know often share amusing or illuminating anecdotes about the song background, contractual obligations, recording situations or other info. These anecdotes can also be put in the comment field, but there is an opportunity here to do more. Context might be more stories than facts, but it's always fun to hear from friends.

Relationships

Great music is made by great musicians, and it can be rewarding to see what else they have done who else they have worked with. Some relationships are surprising and lead to things you might not have explored otherwise. Looking forward to developing this further.

Personal connections

Nothing beats directly supporting artists you like. "I saw them live" expresses both appreciation and commitment. Any connection to the creative process is great and worth sharing, including music purchases and other items that directly benefit artists. Most musicians don't get enough support from streaming. You should be able to see more of how your friends are connecting to the music.

Happenings

New releases, upcoming concerts, and other happenings are important to know about. It's a drag missing something you would have wanted to go see. Friends are good source, but looking forward to connecting with services to get the scoop on what's going on.

Help with setup

Friends also help you get set up. When you add a music friend, Digger checks their library for songs you haven't rated yet and copies their rating info to jump start your tuned listening experience. When you rate your own music you help friends discover music they want to hear. All while you get back into enjoying music in your library.
